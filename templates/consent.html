Decision-Making and Confidence Signals Mission

You are invited to participate in an online research experiment. Before deciding whether to participate, it is important for you to understand the study, what will be expected of you, and any potential risks or benefits.

Purpose of the Study

The purpose of this study is to investigate decision-making under uncertainty in group settings, focusing on how private information, others’ decisions, and confidence cues influence behavior and contribute to information cascades.

Notice

Some elements are intentionally not fully disclosed in advance in order to preserve the validity of the study. A full explanation will be provided during the debriefing at the end.

Procedures

You will be asked to provide Prolific ID. You will then receive instructions regarding the experiment.

Stage 1 – Training Stage: Complete a short set of tasks to ensure you understand the task structure and probabilistic reasoning. Must pass to proceed.

Stage 2 – Main Experiment: Receive a private signal, observe previous decisions, and make choices after seeing additional confidence information.

Risks and Benefits

There are no known physical risks associated with this study. There may be minimal online privacy risks. Benefits include contributing to research and experiencing a realistic decision-making task.

Confidentiality & Payment

Publishing & Voluntary
